ANTISEPTIC

Antiseptic is a substance that prevents or arrests the growth or action of microorganisms by inhibiting their activity or by destroying them. The antiseptic treatment destroys all forms of microbial life and pathogenic microorganisms. The major risk in all medical procedures is the introduction of pathogens that can lead to infection.
Failure to properly disinfect or sterilize equipment carries not the only risk associated with a breach of host barriers but also the risk for person-to-person transmission. Antiseptic treatment is essential mainly for ensuring medical and surgical instruments do not transmit infectious pathogens to the patients.
